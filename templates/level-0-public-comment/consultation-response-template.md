# Consultation Response Template

**Use Case:** Responding to EU "Have Your Say" public consultations

**Time Required:** 2-4 hours (including research)  
**Cost:** €0  
**Deadline:** Varies by consultation (typically 4-12 weeks)  
**Submission:** Web form at ec.europa.eu/info/law/better-regulation/have-your-say

---

## Before You Start

**Find Active Consultations:**
1. Visit "Have Your Say" portal
2. Filter by policy area relevant to you
3. Check deadline (in portal)
4. Download consultation document and supporting materials

**AI Research Prompts:**
```
"Summarize this consultation document focusing on [aspect relevant to me]."
"What are the key questions being asked and what evidence would strengthen responses?"
"Find similar consultations from past 2 years and summarize common themes."
```

## Template Structure

### 1. YOUR DETAILS

**Name/Organization:** [Your name or organization]  
**Country:** [Your country]  
**Category:** [Individual / Small business / Large business / NGO / Public authority / Academic]  
**Email:** [Your contact email]

### 2. EXECUTIVE SUMMARY (2-3 sentences)

[State your main position clearly and concisely]

Example: "As an AI researcher with 10 years experience in computer vision, I support the proposed high-risk categorization but recommend strengthening conformity assessment requirements for biometric identification systems used in public spaces."

### 3. BACKGROUND & EXPERTISE (1 paragraph)

[Briefly establish why your input should be taken seriously]

Example: "I have published 15 peer-reviewed papers on facial recognition systems and currently work on EU-funded research project [name] studying bias in biometric systems across demographic groups."

### 4. SPECIFIC COMMENTS ON CONSULTATION QUESTIONS

**Question 1: [Copy exact question from consultation]**

**Response:**
[Your answer - be specific, cite evidence, reference articles if relevant]

[If you have data/studies supporting your position, reference them: "See Annex A"]

**Question 2: [Copy exact question]**

**Response:**
[Continue for each relevant question]

### 5. ADDITIONAL COMMENTS (Optional)

[Any points not covered by specific questions]

### 6. PROPOSED AMENDMENTS (If Applicable)

**Current Text:** "[Quote relevant passage from draft regulation]"

**Proposed Change:** "[Your suggested rewording]"

**Justification:** "[Why this change would improve the regulation]"

### 7. SUPPORTING EVIDENCE (Annexes)

- Annex A: [Research paper / Technical analysis]
- Annex B: [Case study / Example]
- Annex C: [Additional documentation]

## Tone & Style Guidelines

**DO:**
- ✅ Be specific and evidence-based
- ✅ Cite relevant articles, data, examples
- ✅ Acknowledge complexity and trade-offs
- ✅ Propose constructive solutions
- ✅ Stay focused on substance
- ✅ Use clear, professional language

**DON'T:**
- ❌ Use inflammatory or accusatory language
- ❌ Make broad unsupported claims
- ❌ Focus on politics vs. substance
- ❌ Exceed character limits in web forms
- ❌ Submit identical boilerplate text
- ❌ Wait until last day (technical issues happen)

- [ ] Check compliance with consultation format requirements

- [ ] Review tone for constructive professionalism
- [ ] Identify weak arguments needing more evidence
- [ ] Check citations and references for accuracy
- [ ] Ensure executive summary accurately reflects detailed comments

---

## Submission Checklist

- [ ] Filled out all mandatory fields in web form
- [ ] Answered relevant questions from consultation
- [ ] Attached supporting evidence (if you have it)
- [ ] Kept response focused and under any length limits
- [ ] Saved copy of submission for your records
- [ ] Noted submission date and reference number
- [ ] Set calendar reminder to check if response is published (usually 2-4 weeks)

---

## After Submission

**Track Your Input:**
- Responses typically published on consultation page within 2-4 weeks
- You can see other submissions to compare approaches
- Commission may reference consultation responses in impact assessments
- Your submission becomes public record

**Follow-Up Actions:**
- If consultation leads to legislative proposal, track it on EUR-Lex
- Contact relevant MEPs with policy brief based on your submission
- Consider Ombudsman complaint if consultation process itself was flawed
- Share your experience to help improve this template
