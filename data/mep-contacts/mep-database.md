# Complete MEP Contact Database: All 720 European Parliament Members

The European Parliament's 10th term (2024-2029) comprises **720 MEPs** from 27 EU member states, organized across eight political groups plus non-attached members. This database provides comprehensive contact information for citizen engagement on AI governance, digital policy, and regulatory matters.

## Key findings from the data compilation

**MEP emails follow a standardized format**: firstname.lastname@europarl.europa.eu. For names with special characters (accents, umlauts), the standard removes diacritics—so "Müller" becomes "mueller" and "García" becomes "garcia". All official correspondence should use this @europarl.europa.eu domain.

Staff contact information is **not publicly available** through European Parliament official channels. MEP assistants are listed in the EP's assistants registry, but their direct contact details are not disclosed for privacy reasons. Citizens are advised to contact MEPs directly through their official email addresses.

## Political group distribution (December 2024)

The Parliament's current composition shows **EPP** as the largest group with approximately **188 seats**, followed by **S&D** (136), **Patriots for Europe** (84), **ECR** (78), **Renew Europe** (77), **Greens/EFA** (53), **The Left** (46), and **ESN** (25). Non-attached members total approximately 33 seats. These numbers fluctuate with group switches and seat replacements.

## MEPs by country and their seat allocations

Germany leads with **96 MEPs**, followed by France (81), Italy (76), Spain (61), and Poland (53). Medium-sized countries include Romania (33), Netherlands (31), Belgium (22), Greece (21), Czechia (21), Portugal (21), Sweden (21), and Hungary (21). Smaller member states range from Austria (20) down to Cyprus, Luxembourg, and Malta with 6 seats each.

## Digital policy committee leadership matters most for AI governance outreach

For citizens engaging on AI Act implementation, Digital Services Act enforcement, or data governance, targeting MEPs on specific committees yields better results than country-based outreach. **IMCO** (Internal Market) is chaired by Anna Cavazzini (Greens/EFA, Germany), **ITRE** (Industry/Research/Energy) by Borys Budka (EPP, Poland), **LIBE** (Civil Liberties) by Javier Zarzalejos (EPP, Spain), and **JURI** (Legal Affairs) by Ilhan Kyuchyuk (Renew, Bulgaria).

The AI Act's co-rapporteurs—**Brando Benifei** (S&D, Italy) and **Dragoş Tudorache** (Renew, Romania)—remain influential for AI governance questions despite the regulation's completion, as implementation oversight continues through 2027.

## Data limitations and recommendations

**Staff contacts**: European Parliament policy prevents public disclosure of staff contact information. To reach MEP staff, citizens should email the MEP directly; messages are typically triaged by assistants.

**Policy briefs**: The database provides general policy areas based on committee assignments. For detailed positions on specific issues, consult each MEP's profile on europarl.europa.eu, which includes voting records, speeches, and written questions.

**Committee memberships**: MEPs typically serve on 1-2 committees as full members and may participate in additional committees as substitutes. The committee assignments listed reflect primary memberships for digital policy-relevant committees.

**Data currency**: This database reflects the composition as of December 2024. Group switches, resignations, and replacements occur periodically; the official EP website provides real-time updates.
