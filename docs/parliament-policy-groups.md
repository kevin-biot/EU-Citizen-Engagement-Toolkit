# European Parliament Political Groups (2024-2029)

This overview lists the political groups in the 10th European Parliament term (2024-2029) and their approximate seat counts as of December 2024. Use this as a reference for targeting outreach and understanding broad policy orientations.

## Groups and Orientations

- **EPP – Group of the European People's Party (Christian Democrats)**  
  Approx. 188 seats. Center-right; pro-integration; focus on internal market, security, agriculture, and industrial competitiveness.

- **S&D – Group of the Progressive Alliance of Socialists and Democrats**  
  Approx. 136 seats. Center-left; social protections, workers’ rights, climate action with just transition, digital fairness.

- **Patriots for Europe (PfE)**  
  Approx. 84 seats. Right-wing to far-right; sovereignty-first, restrictive migration policies, skeptical of deeper integration.

- **ECR – European Conservatives and Reformists Group**  
  Approx. 78 seats. Conservative/eurosceptic; national sovereignty, security, fiscal restraint, economic competitiveness.

- **Renew Europe**  
  Approx. 77 seats. Centrist/liberal; pro-business, digital single market, civil liberties, green growth.

- **Greens/EFA – Group of the Greens/European Free Alliance**  
  Approx. 53 seats. Green and regionalist; climate leadership, digital rights, transparency, social inclusion.

- **The Left – The Left group in the European Parliament (GUE/NGL)**  
  Approx. 46 seats. Progressive/left; social justice, civil liberties, anti-austerity, corporate accountability.

- **ESN – Europe of Sovereign Nations Group**  
  Approx. 25 seats. Nationalist/sovereigntist; anti-federalist, restrictive migration, skeptical of EU competences.

- **NI – Non-attached Members**  
  Approx. 33 seats. Individual MEPs not affiliated with a group; positions vary widely.

## How to use this

- Map MEPs to their group to anticipate voting patterns and preferred framing.  
- Pair group orientation with committee roles (e.g., IMCO, ITRE, LIBE) for targeted policy outreach.  
- Track seat changes over time; group switches and replacements occur during the term.  
- For digital/AI policy, prioritize group coordinators and rapporteurs in IMCO, ITRE, LIBE, and JURI.
